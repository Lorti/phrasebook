===Country and territory names===
Country and territory names in Japanese are generally borrowed from their English names and written in katakana. The names of languages are generally formed by adding 語 (''go'') to the end of the country name. Some of the main exceptions are as follows:

;日本 ''Nihon/Nippon'' : Japan
;日本国 ''Nihon-koku'': State of Japan
;アジア ''Ajia'' : Asia
;中国 ''Chūgoku'' : China
;中華人民共和国 ''Chūka jinmin Kyouwa koku'' : People's Republic of China
;台湾 ''Taiwan'' : Taiwan
;香港 ''Honkon'': Hong Kong
;韓国 ''Kankoku'' : South Korea
;大韓民国 ''Daikan Minkoku'' : South Korea
;北朝鮮 ''Kitachōsen'' : North Korea
;朝鮮民主主義人民共和国 ''Chōsen Minsyu syugi Jinmin Kyouwa koku'' : Democratic People's Republic of Korea
